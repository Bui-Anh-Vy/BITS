The majority of pneumonia cases in the community are caused by viruses and bacteria. Pneumonia is a respiratory illness that spreads from person to person through two major routes: direct and indirect. Diabetes

Details

Diabetes is a long-term disease that happens when the pancreas doesn't make enough insulin or when the body doesn't use the insulin it makes well enough. High blood sugar, or hyperglycemia, is caused by diabetes. This is bad for many parts of the body, including the nerves and blood vessels. Cardiovascular manifestations: Angina (pain with pressure between the sternum, often spreading to the chin and shoulder, left hand, shortness of breath, and may be accompanied by sweating or fainting), fatigue on exertion strength, purple. For cerebrovascular disease, there may be 3 signs of stroke: Weakness in the side of the body or limbs, slurred speech or meaningless words, and distorted mouth.

Treatment & Prevention

There are many ways you can control and prevent diabetes as follows: Weight control Increase physical activity Eat healthy vegetables Eat healthy fats Avoid fast dieting No smoking Drink alcohol in moderation Check your sugar regularly


Digestive disorders

Details

Digestion is the process of converting food into compounds that can be taken into the bloodstream via the digestive tract's walls. Digestion starts in the mouth and progresses to the big intestine. A digestive disease is defined as any factor that alters, inhibits, or disrupts food digestion in the gastrointestinal system. This is not an illness, but rather the result of many factors. However, if this problem persists and is not appropriately treated, the patient is more prone to develop digestive-related disorders, most notably intestine cancer. The following are some of the reasons behind this: Stomach diseases caused by colitis Dietary imbalance of gut microbiota Make extensive use of alcoholic drinks.

Treatment & Prevention

Diet: The most common causes of digestive issues are food and water. As a result, it is essential to maintain a healthy diet and choose meals that are easy on the digestive system. Eat prepared meals and drink hot water; avoid foods that are overly spicy, sour, or heavy in protein or fat. Patients suffering from persistent diarrhea should avoid eating a lot of fiber-rich meals. Patients should be given digestive enzymes and liquids that aid digestion. How to avoid Consume healthily, eat prepared meals, drink boiling water, and avoid foods that irritate the digestive tract and cause diarrhea. People who are often constipated should consume extra fiber and green vegetables to aid the body's evacuation function. Limit your use of alcoholic drinks. Probiotics and beneficial bacteria for the digestive system should be supplemented. Scientifically practice the habit of going to the bathroom; every day, go to the toilet 1 time at the same time. Increase your resistance to biological factors that cause digestive issues by taking vitamins and minerals.
